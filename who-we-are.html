Berry Clever

Berry Clever (Rubus peringeniosus) is a contemplative, inquisitive berry of recent domestication.

In the wild, many species can observe, propose, or critique. But Rubus peringeniosus was cultivated differently: through domestication, it developed the ability not only to observe—but to organize, interpret, and make sense of what surrounds it.

Cultivated from diverse wild strains, it can be planted and grafted in any environment. Wherever it grows, it draws from its context—absorbing signals, patterns, and meanings—to produce a richer fruit: one dense with insight.

Its defining property is qualitative research. Because it's not enough to collect data—what matters is extracting its essence.

We don't just gather information. We harvest the why and the how behind human behavior. By combining ethnographic rigor with strategic thinking, we transform complex social narratives into clear, actionable paths for organizations.

Qualitative research

Finding meaning
in data.

In a world shaped by data, we focus on the meaning behind it.

Numbers reveal patterns, trends, and scale. Qualitative research complements this by exploring the contexts, perceptions, and experiences that give those patterns significance.

It is an approach centered on interpretation and understanding. By creating space for people to share their perspectives, it brings forward the social, emotional, and cultural dimensions that influence decisions.

Whether it's a consumer choosing a brand, a person navigating services, or an organization seeking to better understand its communities, qualitative research connects what is observed with why it happens.

Applied research

Where we work.

Qualitative research gains value when it is applied in real contexts. We work across different fields where understanding people—their experiences, decisions, and environments—is essential for making informed and responsible choices.

01 / 03

Social and Humanitarian Contexts

We support organizations in understanding the lived experiences of the communities they serve. This includes areas such as migration, gender, access to services, environmental challenges, and community well-being.

We contribute to public policy analysis and design, as well as monitoring and evaluation processes that seek to understand not only what changes, but how and why those changes are experienced.

02 / 03

Market and Consumer Research

We explore how people relate to products, brands, and services in their daily lives. Beyond preferences, we focus on meanings, habits, and cultural contexts—helping organizations understand the emotional and symbolic dimensions behind consumer behavior.

03 / 03

Institutional and Service Design

We work with institutions and organizations to better understand their processes, systems, and audiences. This includes user experience (UX) research, healthcare experiences, and the design or improvement of services.

By bringing forward people's perspectives, we help make systems more accessible, relevant, and responsive.

We turn situated knowledge into decisions that can be acted upon.
